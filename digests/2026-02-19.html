Research "Comprehension Decay": Why Readable AI Isn't Governable AI
EA Forum ‚Ä¢ February 17, 2026

A new empirical framework called the "Symmetrian Index" tested GPT-4 and Claude across 100+ governance-critical responses. Finding: both models score nearly perfect on comprehensibility (5.0/5) but poorly on reversibility (~2.4/5). Claude scored 0.04/1 on "trace hints" and 0.02/1 on "auditability hooks."

The pattern: "The comprehension is there on the surface, but it decays as soon as you try to go deeper. The outputs are clear. The reasoning is inaccessible."

Implications
This is a direct challenge to the "explainability solves governance" assumption underlying the EU AI Act and NIST frameworks. If readable doesn't mean auditable, current regulatory approaches may be fundamentally misaligned.
